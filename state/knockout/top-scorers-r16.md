# R16 top scorers — Scorito basket

Calibration: expected Scorito points = expected goals × **R16 position multiplier** (DEF/GK **96**, MID **48**, ATT **24** per goal — R16 is the 1.5× stage, per `state/poule-rules.md`). Each alive team plays **one** match, so the goal ceiling is its predicted R16 goals from `state/knockout/predictions-r16.md`; expected goals = team R16 GF × player share of team goals, nudged for penalty/set-piece duty and current tournament form. Submit the top 4; 5–6 are reserves. Re-picked fresh from the 16 alive teams (no carry-forward of eliminated players — the R32 anchors Baena/Spain is eliminated on our card, and Colombia's Muñoz did not add in R32).

**Team goal ceilings this phase (from the locked R16 card):** France 2, Brazil 2, Belgium 2, Argentina 2 (the four multi-goal advancing sides); Morocco 1, England 1, Portugal 1, Colombia 1 (single-goal advancers); losing sides score little and are ignored. The submitted basket sits on the four 2-goal sides plus the one single-goal side that carries a genuine **scoring defender** — Hakimi.

**The lever, sharper again at R16 (multipliers up 50%):** a striker on a 2-goal side banks ~1 goal (24 pts); one set-piece/penalty goal from a defender/mid returns 96/48. The crowd over-picks the strikers (Mbappé, Messi, Vinícius). Our top pick is a **scoring right-back at 96×** — the largest single mispricing on the board even though his team only scores once, because that one goal is worth 4× a striker's and Hakimi is the man who actually takes Morocco's set-pieces and scores them.
