## Champion: England  (winner of group L)

- **Why a genuine contender (title-level profile):** Perfect UEFA qualifying — P8 W8 D0 L0, 22 GF,
  **0 GA, 8 clean sheets**, the best xGA (2.1) of any UEFA side. A tournament is won on a defensive
  base, and England's is elite. Harry Kane (79 England goals, 64-in-56 Bayern season) is a top-tier
  penalty taker and finisher; Bellingham and Saka give knockout-level individual quality. Tuchel's
  structure-first 3-2-5/compact-mid-block side is built to grind out 1-0 / 2-0 knockout wins — exactly
  the profile that survives single-elimination football. Priced **3rd outright (+650/+700)**, i.e. a
  real title side, not a dark horse.

- **Why underrated (the value):** England carries the **3rd-shortest outright price but sits in the
  far softer bracket half**. The market's two co-favourites (Spain ~+450, France ~+475) AND Germany
  AND Belgium are *all* stacked in the **top half** (SF M101) — they will knock each other out before
  the final. England is the best side in the **bottom half** (SF M102), whose other title names are a
  diminished Brazil (worst qualifying in 30 years, Neymar doubtful, Rodrygo + Estêvão out), an ageing
  Argentina, and a narrative-priced Portugal. The crowd anchors on England's "no win since 1966" and
  the Foden/Palmer omissions and ranks them a notch low; the bracket geometry means their realistic
  path to the final never crosses Spain or France. That **P(win) × underpricing** edge — a top-3 side
  that dodged both co-favourites — is the play.

- **Seeded path:** R32 M80 vs **Ecuador (3E)** → R16 M92 vs winner of **Mexico (1A)/Tunisia (3F)**
  (most likely Mexico) → QF M99 vs the **Brazil/Netherlands–Ivory Coast/Senegal** quadrant (most
  likely **Brazil**) → SF M102 vs the **Argentina/Portugal** quadrant (most likely **Argentina**) →
  **Final vs the top-half survivor (Spain or France).**

- **Pivotal tie:** the **SF (M102) vs Argentina**. England beat their group and quadrant comfortably
  on the defensive numbers; the genuine title-level test in their half is the defending champions.
  We back England through it on the structural edge — Tuchel's compact block neutralises a 39-year-old
  Messi being load-managed, and a fully-fit Kane on penalties is the edge in a tight, low-event game
  that 120 minutes + the scoreboard (shootouts don't score in the poule) tends to decide on one moment.

- **Bracket-half read:** England's bottom half (Brazil weakened, Argentina ageing, Portugal narrative-
  priced) is far kinder than the top half, where Spain, France, Germany and Belgium are crammed
  together and must cannibalise each other to reach M101. England **dodge** every co-favourite until
  the final; the sides they **must beat** are Mexico (R16), Brazil (QF) and Argentina (SF) — beatable
  for a side with the tournament's best defensive record.
