# Round 1 predictions

Decider synthesis (Opus). Method per `.claude/skills/predict-round/SKILL.md`: lock the most
defensible toto (protect the 60), then take the most market-underrated *defensible* scoreline
within that direction (chase the +30). Free synthesis of the three advisor lenses (stats /
contrarian / conditions-market) — not a vote; where I diverged from all three or broke a split,
an `Audit` line records it.

## Group A

## Mexico vs South Africa @ Mexico City (alt ~2240m): 2-0
- Result locked (toto): home win — Mexico unbeaten in 8, GA/g 0.9, host crowd of 87k; only direction with a defensible 60.
- Rationale (stats): Mexico 2.0 GF/g, ~40% clean sheets vs a 1.5 GF/g sea-level South African side whose output was padded by weak CAF opposition.
- Value angle: the market shades toward a 2-1/3-1 goal-fest; the underrated shape is the *clean sheet* — a wholly sea-level squad (Foster/Williams fitness doubts) fades in the third quarter at 2240m, so 2-0 over the chalk total.
- Venue/conditions: Azteca altitude is the lever — saps an entirely sea-level South African squad; it caps their goal and keeps the Mexican clean sheet live. All three lenses agree 2-0.

## South Korea vs Czechia @ Zapopan/Guadalajara (alt ~1560m): 2-1
- Result locked (toto): home win — Korea is the demonstrably stronger side (40 GF, unbeaten 16 AFC qualifiers, Son + Lee Kang-in) vs a Czechia that scraped through on two shootouts under a coach with zero prior senior experience.
- Rationale (stats): Korea's transition pace in thinner air outguns a compact Czech block; mild ~1560m altitude hurts the European-profile Czechs more than the Koreans.
- Value angle: Czechia scored 50% of their goals from set pieces and led all UEFA qualifiers in corner goals (Krejci/Schick) — bake in their reply rather than take a tidy 1-0/2-0. 2-1 is the underrated shape.
- Audit: stats said 1-1 (draw, low confidence); contrarian and conditions-market both said 2-1 home win. I sided with the 2-of-3 home-win lean — flipping to a draw risks 60 when Korea is the clearly better qualifier, and the Czech set-piece threat is best honoured *inside* a Korean win (the +30) rather than by handing them a share of the points.
