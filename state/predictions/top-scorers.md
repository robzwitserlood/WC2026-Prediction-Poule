# Group-stage top scorers — Scorito basket

Calibration: expected Scorito points = expected goals × position multiplier (DEF/GK 64, MID 32, ATT 16 per group-stage goal). Ceiling for each player = his team's predicted group GF (round-3 standings); expected goals = team GF × player share of team goals, nudged for penalty/set-piece duty. Submit the top 4; 5–6 are reserves.

The lever: the crowd over-picks marquee strikers (16/goal). Two of our four submitted picks are **midfielders (32/goal)** on free-scoring sides — half the goals for double the points. We keep two nailed-on, high-volume strikers so the basket is not contrarian for its own sake.

## 1. Florian Wirtz — Germany (MID) — SUBMIT
- Our sim: Germany predicted **7** group goals (round-3 standings GF — joint-highest in the field with France).
- Expected goals: **1.5** (≈ 30% of Germany's attacking output per team file; corners + set-piece duty, penalty sharer with Havertz). Over a 7-goal group, 1.5 is conservative for the side's primary creator-finisher.
- Expected points: **~48** (1.5 × 32).
- Value angle: a midfielder on the highest-scoring team in our sim, worth 2× a striker per goal. The crowd pays for Havertz/Musiala up top; Wirtz banks the same names' build-up plus his own dead-ball goals at double the multiplier. Top expected-points pick in the entire basket.

## 2. Kevin De Bruyne — Belgium (MID) — SUBMIT
- Our sim: Belgium predicted **6** group goals (round-3 standings GF — group G winner).
- Expected goals: **1.3** (≈ 21% of Belgium's qualifying goals as joint-top scorer with 6 in qualifying, ~0.75 g/90; **primary penalty + free-kick + corner taker** — Lukaku's fitness is doubtful, concentrating dead-ball goals on KDB).
- Expected points: **~42** (1.3 × 32).
- Value angle: the textbook underpriced pick — a penalty-and-set-piece midfielder (32/goal) on a free-scoring side, with the team's main striker (Lukaku) "out of shape," so the spot-kick and dead-ball goals funnel to him. Crowd files him as a creator, not a scorer.

## 3. Raphinha — Brazil (ATT) — SUBMIT
- Our sim: Brazil predicted **6** group goals (round-3 standings GF — group C winner, +6 GD, e.g. 3-0 vs Haiti in round 2).
- Expected goals: **2.5** (≈ 40–45% of Brazil's goals with Neymar absent; **penalty taker candidate + set-piece taker**; 13 La Liga + 3 UCL goals at club, Brazil's qualifying top scorer with 5).

- Value angle: highest expected-goal volume of any single player we have, on a side our card has scoring six. He carries the pens and set pieces, so his floor is protected even on an off night — the strongest pure-striker pick available without an injury cloud (vs Vinícius's international under-delivery / new central role).

## 4. Kylian Mbappé — France (ATT) — SUBMIT
- Our sim: France predicted **7** group goals (round-3 standings GF — joint-highest, group I winner, 9 pts).

- Value angle: not contrarian, but the highest-ceiling nailed-on striker in the tournament on our joint-top-scoring side — passing him over to chase multipliers would be value-for-its-own-sake. He anchors the basket's floor while picks 1–2 chase the multiplier upside. Diversifies us into a fourth team/group.
