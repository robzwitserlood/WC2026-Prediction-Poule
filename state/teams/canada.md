- System: 4-4-2 or 4-3-3 under Marsch; high-energy pressing, direct play; Canada have made a defensive identity their core strength under Marsch
- Playing style: physical, high-press; transition-based attack; relies on Davies' overlapping runs and David's finishing
- Venue: Canada plays all 3 group games at home — Toronto (June 12 vs Bosnia), Vancouver (June 18 vs Qatar), Vancouver (June 24 vs Switzerland). Significant crowd advantage, familiar grass/conditions.
- No altitude factor for Canada's games — all sea-level North American venues.
- Conditions: June in Toronto and Vancouver is mild and familiar to Canada-based clubs. Good for Canada's fitness.
- Weakness without Davies (opener): left flank loses its primary creative outlet; Bosnia's right side could expose Richie Laryea as a defensive weakness.
- Set-piece threat: Davies when fit is a delivery option; without him, set-piece quality drops.
