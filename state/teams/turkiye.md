## Matchup notes
Style: 4-2-3-1; Çalhanoğlu double pivot with attacking CM; Güler and Yıldız free in final third; wide forwards vs pressing teams. Can be tactically flexible (Montella adaptable from EURO 2024).
Key strength: Güler–Yıldız combination is one of the most dangerous young attacking duos in the tournament. Both 20–21, both in exceptional club form.
Key weakness: concedes regularly (12 GA in 8 qualifiers); 0-6 vs Spain shows vulnerability to elite pressing teams. Defensive shape not world-class.
Climate: squad based in Spain, Italy, England — European leagues. Vancouver (BC Place, climate-controlled) for opener vs Australia — no heat issue match 1. LA (SoFi, open-air, mild) for match 2 vs Paraguay. LA for match 3 vs USA — home crowd pressure.
Set pieces: Güler free kicks are elite; Çalhanoğlu delivery and penalty duty. No dominant aerial CB in set-piece threats.
Historical WC: 2002 — 3rd place. Prior appearances: 1954, 1998.

## Mispricing flag
Two signals:
1. Güler–Yıldız at 20–21 — combined 40 goal involvements at club in 2025-26, both in the best form of their careers — may be systematically underrated by a market that still thinks of Türkiye through a "dark horse" lens. Their attacking quality is not "dark horse" level anymore; it's legitimately among the top 10 at this tournament. If Güler replicates even 40% of his Real Madrid output in 4–5 games, he scores 3–4 tournament goals. The top-scorer market for Güler may be under-priced.
2. Türkiye's defensive fragility (12 GA in 8 qualifiers; 0-6 vs Spain) combined with Group D having USA and Paraguay — both low-block or transition teams — means Türkiye vs USA (Jun 25) could be a high-scoring game (both teams' tendencies: Türkiye attack, USA counter and score). A 2-2 or 3-2 result is statistically plausible and likely under-priced vs the "controlled game" narrative. Group D advance probability at ~73% is roughly correct, but the market likely prices Türkiye as a 1-1 draw team when they're actually more likely to be involved in 3-2 or 2-1 games.
