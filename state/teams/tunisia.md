## Matchup notes
- Lamouchi system: **4-3-3 / 4-1-4-1 / 4-2-3-1** — tactically flexible with two distinct phases. In possession: asymmetric back-3 build-up, high wide fullbacks, inverted wingers tucking inside around target striker. Out of possession: 4-1-4-1 press in final third, drops to compact **4-4-2 mid-to-low block** if bypassed — prioritises plugging central channels, forcing wide where CBs can clear.
- Tunisia's edge is defensive compactness and set-piece discipline. Their strength is frustrating opponents and hitting on the counter.
- **Heat at Monterrey (R1 and R2 both at Estadio BBVA):** Tunisia play R1 (Jun 14) and R2 (Jun 20) in open-air Monterrey. As a North African squad, many players are from Tunisian and Middle Eastern leagues — heat-adapted. This is a genuine physical advantage vs Sweden (R1) and Japan (R2). R3 in Kansas City (Arrowhead, outdoor, heat) is also warm.
- WC2022 defensive record: 1 GA in 3 matches (best among African teams at that tournament); beat France 1-0 (rotated squad). Low-scoring games are Tunisia's identity.
- Attacking weakness vs top opposition: 0 goals in 2 warm-ups vs Austria and Belgium (0-1, 0-5). The Belgium result (0-5) is damaging evidence of attacking impotence and structural fragility against high-quality opponents.
- Counter-attack model requires compact positioning to launch — hard to execute if going behind early.
- Lamouchi inexperience as Tunisia head coach (2 games) is a tactical preparation risk.

## Mispricing flag
**Tunisia's qualifying record is structurally misleading.** The market gives Tunisia ~40-48% qualification odds, partly because the expanded format rewards good third-place finishers. However: (1) 0 GF and 6 GA in 2 warm-up friendlies vs European opposition (Austria, Belgium) — including a 0-5 thrashing — suggests the attacking thin-ness behind the top-scorer-excluded Ben Romdhane is severe; (2) Lamouchi has 2 matches of head-coaching experience — tactical cohesion is genuinely uncertain; (3) their defensive record in CAF qualifying is inflated by near-zero competition quality. **Fade Tunisia as a qualification bet at +140 — the European warm-up results are a credible signal of a team that may struggle to score at all.** The one genuine value on Tunisia: **as underdogs vs Sweden in 35°C Monterrey heat (+120), their compact defensive block + heat adaptation gives them a realistic draw outcome** that the market may underweight.
