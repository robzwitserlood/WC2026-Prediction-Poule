## Matchup notes
- System: 4-4-2 (compact defensive mid-block; double pivot M. Caicedo + Franco)
- Counter-attacking; low block absorbs pressure and transitions fast through Estupiñán and right-side pace
- Hincapié + Pacho CB pairing: elite defensive duo; not easily beaten aerially or on the ground
- Set-piece danger: Pacho aerial, Estupiñán delivery — but only 14 goals in 18 qualifiers means set pieces not prolific
- Key vulnerability: Low goal output means they need to be near-perfect defensively; any goal conceded forces them to break their structure
- Historical WC: 2002 (group exit), 2006 (R16 — best ever), 2014 (group exit); 2022 group exit (but qualified as hosts, effectively)
- Heat: Quito home matches played at 2,850m altitude — paradoxically, heat/humidity in US Gulf venues (Houston, Kansas City) is physically different to altitude but Ecuador's cardiovascular conditioning from altitude training gives endurance advantage. Research suggests altitude-trained teams handle heat better than sea-level teams.
- "Surprise package" narrative: Several analysts note Ecuador's squad is the most talented in history (Caicedo, Hincapié, Pacho, Estupiñán all at elite European clubs).

## Mispricing flag
**Ecuador's defense (13 clean sheets in 18 qualifiers, 0.28 GA/g) is statistically elite — one of the best defensive units at the tournament.** Yet their offense is among the weakest (0.78 GF/g in qualifying). The market prices them as a strong 2nd-place finisher but likely underestimates two scenarios: (1) **Ecuador 0-0 or 1-0 vs Germany** — Germany's porous defense (3 conceded vs Switzerland, 1 vs USA) is exposed by a disciplined counter unit; a draw or Ecuador win would be a significant value bet. (2) **Ecuador is overpriced as a comfortable 2nd** — Ivory Coast's recent form (beat France 2-1) and heat advantage might flip that battle. Ecuador's attacking threat is low enough that Ivory Coast at +250 (vs Ecuador -145) looks like potential value.
