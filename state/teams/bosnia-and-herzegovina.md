# Bosnia and Herzegovina  (group B)

## Form (last ~12 matches, 2024 – Jun 2026)
UEFA qualifying Group H: finished 2nd behind Austria; missed automatic qualification. Key qualifying matches included draws and wins vs lower-tier opponents.
Qualifying stats: 19 GF 9 GA across 10 qualifying matches = 1.9 GF/g, 0.9 GA/g. Scored in all 10 qualifying games. Conceded in 7 of 10. Source: Tribuna.com, 2025.
Playoff semi (vs Wales, Mar 2026): won on penalties (Dzeko's dramatic equalizer in extra time).
Playoff final (vs Italy, Mar 2026): 1-1 AET, won 4-1 on pens. **Beating 4-time champions Italy to qualify** — historic result. Source: Al Jazeera, Mar 2026.
Post-playoff friendlies: drew vs North Macedonia (draw), drew vs Panama (draw) — inconsistent.
Pre-tournament: fans welcomed in Toronto ahead of opener, Jun 8. Source: WakingTheRed, Jun 2026.

## Matchup notes
- System: 4-2-3-1 built around Dzeko; Benjamin Tahirovic and Armin Gigovic as double-pivot; direct service to Dzeko in the box; physical and high-energy pressing
- Playing style: transitional, counter-pressing; direct balls into the striker; not possession-based
- **Without Dzeko**: Demirovic leads the line but Bosnia lose their focal point, physical and psychological anchor, and most prolific scorer
- Venue: Canada opener in Toronto (June 12) — Bosnia face a partisan Canadian crowd without their captain fit
- Climate/conditions: All Group B games at sea level or low altitude (Toronto, Los Angeles, Seattle). Fine for Bosnia's European-based players.
- Set-piece delivery: Dzeko is the primary aerial threat from set pieces; without him, Bosnia's set-piece danger drops sharply

## Mispricing flag

If Dzeko is confirmed absent or plays limited minutes across the group stage, Bosnia's goal expectation drops from ~1.9/g to ~1.2/g. Their primary threat metric (Dzeko carrying 50%+ of goals) collapses without him. **Bosnia at +650 to win the group could be slightly too SHORT if Dzeko misses entirely** (Canada and Switzerland would be clear 1-2). Conversely, **if Dzeko starts vs Canada and plays 60-70 mins, Bosnia have legitimate upset potential** against a Davies-less Canada opening in front of a hostile Toronto crowd. The Bosnia vs Canada scoreline is the most uncertain match in either group — both teams have significant injury absences for the opener.
