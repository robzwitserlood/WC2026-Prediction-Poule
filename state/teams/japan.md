## Matchup notes
- System: 3-4-2-1 (high-press, compact transitions; inverted wing-backs)
- Style: High-intensity pressing into rapid transitions; collective intelligence over star power; proven low-block vs. superior opposition (beat England, Scotland, Brazil)
- Strength: Transitions from defense (wing-back overlaps + Kubo cutting inside); headed goal volume (12 in qualifying) — aerial set pieces an underrated danger
- Vulnerability: Mitoma and Minamino absences weaken left-side creativity; Endo fitness is central to midfield structure; if Endo is below 100%, transition pressing is disrupted
- Can absorb pressure and counter: demonstrated 1-0 wins vs top nations with minimal possession
- Historical WC: 2010 (R16), 2018 (R16), 2022 (R16 on pens vs Croatia after beating Germany and Spain) — historically overperform in group stage vs top opponents
- Heat/conditions: Osaka league (J-League) mostly played in humid conditions; Kubo and others from La Liga (hot Spain); reasonable heat adaptation. But US summer venues (Atlanta, Miami range) could be extreme. Japan staff known for rigorous conditioning protocols.
- Travel: Long haul (Tokyo to US); Japan typically arrives early for acclimatization.
