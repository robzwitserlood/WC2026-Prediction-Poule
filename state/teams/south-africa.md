## Matchup notes
- System: 4-4-2 under Hugo Broos; compact, counter-attacking, disciplined defensively
- Playing style: technically gifted, physically "lightweight"; Broos emphasizes tactical discipline to bridge physicality gap; quick, direct transitions
- Altitude vulnerability: entire squad is CAF/sea-level based (Sundowns, Burnley, European clubs all at low altitude). Opening game at Estadio Azteca (2,240 m) is a significant fitness test.
- Set-piece threat: limited — no data shows specific set-piece prowess; goals were spread across open play in qualifying
- Strength: counter-attack off Appollis and Foster pace; defensive organization when sitting deep
- First WC appearance since hosting in 2010. WC 2010: P3 W0 D1 L2, 3 GF 5 GA — group exit.

## Mispricing flag
South Africa are correctly priced as longshots at +1200 for the group. The FLAGS in the data:
1. **Ronwen Williams fitness** is under-reported by general bettors; if he is not 100%, South Africa's defensive organization collapses.
2. **Foster fitness** is also ambiguous — a semi-fit Foster drastically reduces their goal threat.
3. The **altitude game vs Mexico** is structurally bad for them: sea-level squad, first game, home crowd of 87,000. Their 1.5 GF/g in qualifying was largely against weaker CAF opponents.
4. Under-the-radar upside: if Foster is fit and Williams is sharp, a scrappy 1-1 draw with Mexico is more likely than +550 implies — South Africa are not 5:1 underdogs on form alone. **Potential value: South Africa draw vs Mexico (around +330) or South Africa to score in the opener.**
