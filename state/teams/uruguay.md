# Uruguay  (group H)

## Form (last ~10 competitive matches)
CONMEBOL qualifying (18 matches total, 2023–Sep 2025): W7 D7 L4, 22 GF, 12 GA. Finished 4th.
- Standout results: 2-0 vs Brazil (Oct 2023); 2-0 vs Argentina at La Bombonera (Nov 2023)
- Difficult patch: 4 winless in a row (Sep–Oct 2024), including three 0-0 draws + 1-0 loss in Peru
- Recovery: 2-0 vs Venezuela (Jun 2025), 3-0 vs Peru (Sep 4 2025), 0-0 vs Chile (Sep 9 2025)
Recent friendlies (pre-tournament cycle): W 2-1 vs Uzbekistan; D 0-0 vs Mexico; L 1-5 vs USA; D 1-1 vs England (Mar 2026, Valverde pen); D 0-0 vs Algeria
Pre-tournament form: three draws and a 5-1 thrashing in last four friendlies; scored just twice.
No June 2026 warm-up matches scheduled before WC opener Jun 15.

## Scoring profile
- CONMEBOL qualifying: 22 GF / 18 = 1.22 GF/g; 12 GA / 18 = 0.67 GA/g
- Fantasy Football Scout stats: 0.78 GF/g, 0.56 GA/g (source counts 14 GF / 10 GA from 18 Q matches — slightly different cut; either way sub-1 GF/g)
- Clean-sheet rate in qualifying: 10/18 = 55.6%
- Typical winning margin: narrow (1-goal wins common); capable of 2-0 or 3-0 but also blanked in 8 of final 12 qualifiers
- Failed to score in 8 of last 12 qualifying matches — significant attacking-output concern

## Goal threats
- **Darwin Nunez** (ATT, penalties uncertain; set pieces no): 5 qualifying goals in 18 apps (~23% of team's qualifying GF); top qualifier scorer. BUT has not played for Al-Hilal since February 2026 — serious match-sharpness deficit entering tournament. Leads the line on physique and pace but fitness/sharpness question is live.
- **Federico Valverde** (MID, penalties YES — converted vs England Mar 2026 + Copa America shootouts; set pieces YES — corners/FKs): ~9 goals in 73 caps; 20 goal involvements for Real Madrid in 2025-26 season. Uruguay's engine and most reliable match-winner. Primary penalty taker. Carries outside forward/box-to-box role; accounts for substantial share of team's creative output.
- **Giorgian de Arrascaeta** (MID/ATT, set pieces YES — corners + FKs delivery): Flamengo; creative No.10; faces calf problem uncertainty but included in squad. De Arrascaeta + Nicolas de la Cruz have baked-in club understanding from Flamengo.
- **Nicolas de la Cruz** (MID): 3 qualifying goals; Flamengo partner of de Arrascaeta; can score from midfield positions.

## Matchup notes
- System: 4-2-3-1 base; shifts 3-1-3-1 in possession with No.6 dropping deeper and fullbacks bombing forward.
- Press-and-counter: relentless high press, profit from turnovers, pacey wingers on transition.
- Set pieces: Valverde and de Arrascaeta on corners/FKs; Valverde on penalties. Araújo and Giménez aerial threats.
- Weakness: goalless in 8 of final 12 qualifying games; narrow attacking pool if Nunez not sharp; fullback quality below elite-team level; 5-1 loss vs USA showed tactical rigidity when Bielsa made "like-for-like changes rather than adapting system" against overlapping fullbacks.
- Conditions — HEAT/HUMIDITY (Miami, R1 Jun 15 and R2 Jun 21): Uruguay is based in Cancun at sea level; players come from European leagues (low-heat environments). A player "left halftime and suffered dizziness/dehydration" in a 2024 Miami match. Assessed as one of the teams with highest combined environmental burden at WC2026. Two Miami matches in tropical summer heat is a genuine fitness risk.
- Conditions — ALTITUDE (Guadalajara, R3 Jun 26, ~1560m): Uruguay is a low-lying country with no altitude-adapted players; training at sea level in Cancun. Playing Spain at altitude after two heat-drained matches is structurally the worst environmental sequence of any Group H team. This is a crowd/market-underrated factor.

## Mispricing flag
OVERPRICED as group runner-up relative to their attacking output. The market prices Uruguay as clear second at ~+375, but the attacking profile is deeply concerning: 0.78 GF/g in qualifying, blanked in 8/12 late qualifiers, star striker (Nunez) without competitive football since February, midfield creative hub (de Arrascaeta) doubted for opener. The defence (Araujo–Gimenez) is genuinely elite and the clean-sheet rate (55.6%) is real. Uruguay's profile is: grind out 0-0s and 1-0s, not score 2+ per game. The severe environmental burden (two Miami heat games + altitude finale) is structurally the worst sequence of any Group H team and is crowd-underrated. Value against Uruguay covering a -1 or -1.5 handicap in either Miami match. Conversely, Uruguay's defensive solidity makes them legitimate to back for low-scoring results (U2.5 goals) in all three matches.
