## Matchup notes
- System: 4-2-3-1 or 4-3-3; organized, structured, efficient; Yakin's Switzerland are not spectacular but extremely hard to beat
- Playing style: defensive solidity first, exploit transitions; Ndoye's pace on the right is a key weapon; Xhaka controls tempo in midfield
- Heat/conditions: Swiss players based primarily in Bundesliga and EPL — accustomed to cold/mild weather. June in Vancouver (24-26°C) and Group B venues is manageable but warmer than Swiss football conditions.
- Altitude: All Group B games at sea level — no altitude issue.
- Set-piece threat: Xhaka takes free kicks and penalties; Akanji is an aerial threat at corners from the back.
- Historical group stage ceiling: Switzerland advance but historically stall at the Last 16 (haven't won a knockout game since 1954).
- Schedule: opens vs Qatar (Jun 13), then Bosnia (Jun 18, LA), then Canada (Jun 24, Vancouver). Favorable opener vs weakest group opponent.

The genuine mispricing question is at the tournament level: **+12500 at 0.79% implied — is that too long or correct?** Their defensive record (2 GA in 6 qualifying games, Kobel + Akanji as the spine) is legitimate. The 1-6 Qatar 2022 exit to Portugal showed their ceiling against elite opposition, but Group B offers an easy path.
**Key value angle for scorelines**: Switzerland vs Qatar opener is likely a **comfortable Switzerland win (2-0 or 3-0)** — Qatar's 0W in 5 (1 GF, 6 GA) under Lopetegui makes them extremely vulnerable to an organized Swiss side. The market will price this as a routine win; the correct score 2-0 Switzerland carries value.
