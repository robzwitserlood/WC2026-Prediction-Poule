## Goal threats
- **Jose Fajardo** (ATT/FW): joint top scorer in third-round qualifying (3 goals); scored match-winner at El Salvador (only goal) and late winner vs Guatemala (3-2); age 32, experienced; rises for big moments. Share of team's WC qualifying GF: ~33%.
- **Adalberto Carrasquilla** (MID/CAM, set pieces YES — primary creative hub, corners and FK delivery): Pumas UNAM; creative No.10 and primary attacking organiser; NOT a high volume scorer but drives all attacking build-up. INJURY CONCERN: groin injury suffered May 24 in Liga MX final (Cruz Azul 2-1). Has NOT joined group training as of Jun 10; completing differentiated sessions. Coach says "advancing very favorably" but TUDN confirmed "still in doubt" for opener vs Ghana (Jun 17). May miss Ghana match; targeting Croatia (Jun 23).
- **Cecilio Waterman** (ATT/FW): 2 qualifying goals; 34 years old; powerful striker competing with Fajardo for starting berth; Fajardo slightly ahead in selection race.
- **Amir Murillo** (DEF/RWB, set pieces YES — delivers from right flank): Süper Lig regular; primary right-flank threat; free-kick and corner delivery option; overlapping runs from wingback role.
- **Set-piece note**: Panama's most consistent attacking platform is set pieces — Murillo delivery, Carrasquilla when fit, with physical bodies (Godoy area) contesting aerials. If Carrasquilla is absent, set-piece quality drops materially.

## Matchup notes
- System: 4-2-3-1 primary; can shift to 3-4-3/3-5-2. Double pivot = Godoy + Martinez. Carrasquilla as No.10. Murillo high from right.
- Physical, organised, defensive-first base. Built to be hard to beat rather than to dominate possession.
- Attack: set pieces are primary goal vehicle; secondary route is counter-attack via Fajardo/Waterman pace in behind on transition.
- Pressing: physical pressing under Christiansen, fast transitions through the flanks.
- Weakness: limited build-up quality if Carrasquilla absent; 6-2 loss to Brazil showed they can be opened up by elite pressing sides; 37-year-old Godoy anchor has reduced physical output; squad ceiling limited vs England/Croatia.
- Conditions — TORONTO (R1, Jun 17 vs Ghana, BMO Field): June in Toronto is mild/temperate, 15–22°C. This suits Panama's Central American players better than northern European opponents. Panama actually has a climate advantage over Ghana in Toronto (both tropical-origin nations, but Toronto June is cooler than both home climates; neither team is disadvantaged here).
- Conditions — FOXBOROUGH MA (R2, Jun 23 vs Croatia): Northeast US, mid-June, similar mild conditions. No notable environmental edge.
- Conditions — EAST RUTHERFORD NJ (R3, Jun 27 vs England, MetLife): Similar Northeast US June conditions. No significant heat or altitude factor for any Group L match — all venues are sea level with moderate June temperatures.
- Familiarity: Panama's MLS-heavy and CONCACAF-based players are mostly North-America-familiar. Godoy spent decade in MLS (San Jose, Nashville). This is a marginal advantage over teams unfamiliar with North American summer logistics.

## Mispricing flag
POTENTIALLY UNDERPRICED in the Ghana match specifically. Panama's qualifying record (unbeaten, 1.50 GF/g, 0.67 GA/g in third round) is considerably stronger than their +5000 group-winner odds suggest. Ghana is in transition (Queiroz era, uneven AFCON/qualifying form) and the teams are closer in quality than the market implies. Panama +5000 to win the group is correctly priced — they won't win the group. But Panama vs Ghana (Jun 17) as a standalone match should be much tighter than odds suggest: Panama have the defensive structure, set-piece threat, and familiar continental conditions to grind a 1-0 or 0-0. If Carrasquilla is fit for Jun 17, Panama's first-match value as draw or win vs Ghana is materially underpriced at current -market favorite prices for Ghana (~-130 to -150). Monitor Carrasquilla fitness in the 72 hours before kickoff — his availability is the single biggest binary swing for Panama's chances.
