## Matchup notes
- System: compact, organized; likely 4-2-3-1 or 4-4-2 block; relies heavily on set-piece delivery
- Playing style: "hard running, set-pieces, togetherness" (Opta); not technically flamboyant; wins through dead-ball proficiency and individual Schick quality
- Altitude: Group A games in Guadalajara (sea-level equivalent, fine for Czechia) for the first match (vs South Korea Jun 11), then South Africa (Atlanta, sea-level), then Mexico City (Azteca, 2,240m). Final group game at altitude will be a challenge.
- Conditions: Most Czech players are in German Bundesliga / English PL / La Liga — accustomed to cold/wet; North American heat and humidity in June (Guadalajara ~32°C) could be a factor.
- Set-piece corner delivery: Czechia's primary attacking threat — teams who concede a lot from corners are especially vulnerable. South Korea and South Africa both had mixed defensive records vs set pieces.
- Vulnerability: Only had their playoff games and a warm-up under the new manager; tactical cohesion is a genuine unknown; away defeats in qualifying suggest fragility against high-quality opposition.

## Mispricing flag
Czechia at +320 to win Group A is suspicious — they are priced equivalent to South Korea despite:
1. South Korea went unbeaten in 16 AFC qualifiers (40 GF, 8 GA) vs Czechia's scrappy playoff qualification via two shootouts.
2. Czechia's best qualifying result was against weaker opponents; they were hammered 5-1 by Croatia.
3. New manager with zero senior experience pre-December 2025.
**Czechia's set-piece threat is REAL and underrated for scoreline prediction** — expect them to score from corners against every opponent. But for group win probability, they look slightly overpriced relative to South Korea. Their most exploitable angle: **Krejci (DEF) is a viable Scorito scorer play** — defender scoring from corners is maximum-value in position-weighted Scorito scoring.
