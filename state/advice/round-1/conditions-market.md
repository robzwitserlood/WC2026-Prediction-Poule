# Round 1 advice — conditions-market lens

Lens rule: I weight venue (altitude/heat/humidity/indoor-AC), travel, host edge, and the bookmaker-vs-stats mispricing gap above raw form. Where heat or altitude is neutralised by an AC roof, the conditions edge vanishes and I revert to the cleaner quality read. Where the crowd overrates a cool-climate squad in a hot/high spot — or underrates an acclimatised one — I lean into it on the scoreline shape, never flipping a defensible toto.

- Confidence: high
- Why (through the conditions-market lens): the textbook altitude+host trap — Azteca 2240m + 87k home crowd vs an entirely sea-level South African squad (Sundowns/Burnley/Europe) in their opener; their 1.5 GF/g was vs weak CAF sides, and the third-quarter altitude fade plus Williams/Foster fitness clouds make a clean Mexico win the structurally correct shape, market +550 SA underrates the venue.
