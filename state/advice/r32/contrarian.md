# R32 advice — contrarian lens

_Stage: R32 (1× — 60 toto / 90 exact). Penalties decide advancement but do NOT score: a 120-min draw is a real, scoreable outcome and my prime underpriced bucket. R32 working base rates (history §1.8): ET ~30-35%, PSO ~15-18%, favourite advances ~70% → roughly one upset in every 3-4 ties is the structural expectation._

## Lens framing — where the crowd is wrong, and where it isn't

My lens has been the **weakest** all tournament (56% toto, miscalibrated low≥high in two rounds). The lesson is **discipline, not reflexive contrarianism**: I only flip a result direction where the stats genuinely compel it, and I lean on the **draw** (which banks the toto AND a clean exact the field skips) rather than naked underdog flips. The crowd over-picks favourites to win in **90 minutes** and forgets penalties don't score — so the value is concentrated in **cagey, well-matched, two-strong-defence ties going level after 120**.

The four places the market is genuinely wrong this round:
1. **Netherlands vs Morocco (M76)** — the only outright-flip I'll plant a flag on. NED's back line is gutted (de Ligt, de Vrij, Timber, Schouten all OUT); Morocco are a compact set-piece machine who *already* knocked Spain out on pens at this exact stage in 2022. The crowd prices the orange shirt, not the defence behind it.
2. **Portugal vs Croatia (M84)** — the textbook underpriced draw: two elite PSO sides, Portugal just blanked by Colombia's block, Croatia the counter-punching low-block specialists with Livakovic (3 saves vs Japan 2022). The market wants a Ronaldo winner; 120 minutes says level.
3. **Spain vs Austria (M83)** — Spain shorn of Nico Williams (+Pino doubt), scored only 5 in the group (0-0 vs Cape Verde), and own the **worst WC PSO record in football** (4 losses, scored 0 vs Morocco). Austria's Rangnick press + Kalajdzic/Sabitzer set-pieces make a draw far more live than -450 implies.
4. **Mexico vs Ecuador (M79)** and **Brazil vs Japan (M74)** — two low-scoring sides each; Ecuador's elite CB pair (Hincapie/Pacho) beat Germany, Japan beat Brazil 1-0 in June and Brazil are without Raphinha. Both are draw-shaped, not comfortable-favourite-shaped.

Where the crowd is **right** and I refuse to fight it (chalk conceded, low/med conf): Argentina-Cape Verde, Colombia-Ghana, France-Sweden, Germany-Paraguay, Belgium-Senegal, USA-Bosnia. Forcing contrarian calls into these is exactly how my lens bled points.
