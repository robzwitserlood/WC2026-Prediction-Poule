# R32 advice — stats lens

_Stage: r32 (×1 multiplier — 60 toto / 90 exact, confirmed vs poule-rules.md). Lens: form/xG purist._

## Lens framing

The stats lens derives every 120-minute scoreline from each side's **actual group-stage scoring/conceding rates**, recent form, and the specific matchup — then takes the statistically-favoured shape even when it is the chalk. Two structural corrections from the history reference apply tournament-wide: (1) knockout football is **cagier and lower-scoring** than group output — Tier 1 puts ET-match 120-min averages at ~2.3 goals and regulation KO at ~2.6–2.8, so I shade group goal totals **down** a notch; (2) the R32 analogue (Euro R16) gives **~70% favourite-advance, ~30–35% ET, ~15–18% PSO** — meaning roughly one upset in three-to-four ties and a real (but minority) draw bucket. I lock the toto first (the +50% exact bonus is never worth flipping the result direction), then pick the most likely exact shape inside it. Where two strong defences or two well-matched sides meet, the 1-1 / 0-0 draw is the statistically-correct call and I name the shootout winner by penalty record.

## Biggest convictions (stake the toto)

1. **M78 France 3-1 Sweden** and **M75 Germany 2-0 Paraguay** — the two cleanest attack-vs-weak-defence gaps in the round; France (10 GF / 2 GA) and Germany (10 GF) face sides that either qualified 3rd on counters or scored 1 group goal. High confidence on result and shape.
2. **M87 Argentina 3-0 Cape Verde** — maximum-points group winner with 0 GA vs a three-draw debutant; the squad-value and form gap is the largest in the field. Vozinha could nick it to 2-0 but the toto is not in doubt.
3. **M88 Colombia 2-0 Ghana** and **M80 England 2-0 DR Congo** — both favourites have a real defensive base against opponents with chronic WC clean-sheet problems (Ghana 0 in 10, DRC 0 in WC history). Tuchel/Lorenzo sides produce exactly the 2-0 shape.

## Biggest uncertainties (low-confidence calls)

1. **M84 Portugal-Croatia (1-1, Portugal pens)** — my hardest call. Two cagey sides genuinely heading to extra time; Croatia's perfect 4-0 WC shootout record (Livakovic) is the orthodox shootout pick and I am overriding it only on the aging-core/fresh-Ronaldo read. A 0-0 and a Croatia-advances outcome are both very live.
2. **M76 Netherlands-Morocco (1-1, Netherlands pens)** — the Dutch defensive injuries make this the most vulnerable favourite; Morocco's block + a genuinely live shootout (Dutch 2-6 record) means Morocco advancing is a real outcome. Tight either way.
3. **M86 Australia-Egypt (1-1, Egypt pens)** — entirely hinged on Salah's hamstring. Salah-out makes the draw and an Australia shootout plausible; Salah-in makes it a narrow Egypt regulation win. The single biggest fitness swing in the round.
4. **M79 Mexico-Ecuador (1-0 Mexico)** — two elite low-scoring defences; the altitude edge is real but 0-0/pens is a coin-flip alternative, and Mexico's "curse of the fifth game" history adds noise.
