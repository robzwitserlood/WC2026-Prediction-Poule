## Lens framing — what knockout-context factors actually move R32

This is the FIRST knockout round, so two of my usual levers are muted and two are amplified:

- **Suspensions: almost a non-factor this round.** Every group-stage red (Mexico's Montes, Paraguay's Almiron, Belgium's Ngoy, Bosnia's Muharemovic, S. Africa's Sithole/Zwane) was *served inside the group stage*, and yellow cards reset after the group phase. So nobody is missing R32 to a ban. This is unusual — suspensions become live again only from R16 onward (a fresh yellow in R32 can carry). I note it because the decider should NOT down-weight any side for a "suspension" here; there are none.
- **Injuries are the dominant live variable.** Star availability swings five ties: **Salah (Egypt, hamstring — doubt)**, **Nico Williams (Spain, adductor — out/limited)**, **Raphinha (Brazil, thigh — doubt)**, **Kudus (Ghana, out all tournament)**, **Schlotterbeck (Germany, out)**, **Pulisic (USA, calf — returning but not sharp)**.
- **Penalty / extra-time temperament (Tier 2) is my biggest edge vs pure stats.** R32 base rates (analogised from Euro R16): ~30–35% ET, ~15–18% PSO. Where I call a 120-min draw, the shootout winner is driven by record, not the 120-min favourite. The standout priors: **Croatia 4-0 all-time WC PSO + Livakovic** (3 saves vs Japan 2022); **Spain worst major-nation PSO record** (4 WC losses, scored 0 vs Morocco 2022); **Belgium's "Golden Generation, no trophies" KO fragility**; **Brazil's QF/SF PSO wobble** (missed vs Croatia 2022); **Japan 0-2 in WC PSOs** (avoid as a shootout pick — they equalise via open play, they don't win shootouts); **Argentina 6-1, Emi Martinez**; **Senegal AFCON 2022 winners incl. a PSO** but no WC PSO sample.
- **Knockout DNA / big-game tactics.** Compact, elimination-savvy sides that LOWER their goal output in knockouts: **Morocco** (2022 SF run, 1 open-play goal conceded through the semis), **Paraguay** (beat Japan on pens 2010, deep block), **Croatia** (2018 final, 2022 SF — thrive in tight games), **Ecuador** (elite low block, 0.28 GA/g qualifying), **Japan** (beat Germany + Spain 2022 from a low block). First-timers / poor-KO sides whose ceiling is capped: **Cape Verde** (first WC, zero KO/penalty pedigree), **DR Congo** (first WC since 1974), **Ghana** (1 win in last 7 WC matches, 0 clean sheets in 10 WC games, and now Kudus-less).

Net tilt: I lean toward **lower-scoring lines and more draws-to-penalties than the chalk**, and where two elimination-hardened defences meet I take the 120-min draw and name the temperament-favoured shootout winner.

## Top 3 ties where knockout-context most changes the call vs pure stats

1. **Belgium vs Senegal (M81) — Senegal advance on pens.** Pure stats lean Belgium (better squad value, 5-1 demolition of NZ). Knockout-context flips the *shootout*: Belgium's trophyless Golden-Generation KO history vs Senegal's AFCON-2022-winning temperament + Mendy is a real, exploitable, market-ignored prior. The draw toto plus Senegal-advances is my boldest contrarian-temperament read.

2. **Portugal vs Croatia (M84) — draw, Croatia advance on pens.** Stats see a coin-flip; temperament does not. Croatia's perfect 4-0 WC PSO record + Livakovic (3 saves vs Japan 2022) is the single strongest shootout prior in the entire bracket, against a Portugal side with no WC PSO sample that was just stifled 0-0 by Colombia. The history reference makes this call for me.

3. **Spain vs Austria (M83) — draw, but Spain win inside 120 (NOT pens).** The nuance is the point: Spain's catastrophic WC PSO record (0 scored vs Morocco 2022) + the Williams/Pino injuries means a draw that reaches a shootout is genuine Austria-upset territory. My read is that Spain's open-play class breaks Rangnick's press before pens — so I bank the underrated *draw shape* while keeping Spain advancing, and explicitly flag that if it DOES reach a shootout, Austria are live well beyond their price.
