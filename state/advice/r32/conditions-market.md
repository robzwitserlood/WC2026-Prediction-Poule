# R32 advice — conditions-market lens

_Stage: r32 (1× multiplier — 60 toto / 90 exact, confirmed in poule-rules.md). Penalties decide advancement but do NOT score. I lock the 120-min toto, then weight venue/altitude/heat/travel and the bookmaker-vs-stats gap to pick the scoreline shape and (on any draw) the shootout winner._

## Lens framing — what conditions & the market are actually pricing in this R32

**1. The one true venue edge: Azteca altitude (M79).** Mexico City sits at 2,240 m. Mexico played all three group games at altitude and stay there; Ecuador are the *one* opponent in the field who partly neutralise it (Quito home base 2,850 m — altitude-trained cardiovascular). This is the only match where altitude is decisive, but the crowd over-applies it (treating Mexico as a lock) while under-pricing Ecuador's elite defence (3 GA across the group, beat Germany). The real read: altitude helps Mexico *late* (3rd-quarter fade for visitors), but Ecuador are the worst possible draw for an altitude bully because they don't fade.

**2. Heat is mostly NEUTRALISED by venue/kickoff this round — don't double-count it.** The crowd's instinct ("hot venue = African/South-American edge") is half-wrong here because of *domes and night kickoffs*:
- **M76 Netherlands–Morocco, Monterrey**: a **9:00 PM ET night game**. June Monterrey daytime heat is brutal, but a night kickoff strips most of it. Morocco's edge here is squad construction (European-based, compact block) + Netherlands' shredded back line — NOT heat. I will not over-weight conditions here.
- **M74 Brazil–Japan (Houston, NRG) and M85 Switzerland–Algeria (Vancouver, BC Place) and M77/M86 (AT&T Arlington)** are **domes/retractable roofs** → climate-controlled. Heat is a non-factor; this actively *helps* the cool-climate side the crowd fears for. Vancouver's dome suits Algeria's technical game (indoor-European feel) but removes any Swiss heat disadvantage.
- Genuine open-air heat games: **M80 England–DR Congo (Atlanta, July 1 noon)** and **M87 Argentina–Cape Verde (Miami, July 3 evening)**. Atlanta noon in July is the single hottest slot — a real, under-priced edge for DR Congo's African-conditioned legs over an end-of-long-season England.

**3. The squad-availability re-pricing the market is slow on.** Knockout team-sheets just changed and books anchor to group form:
- **Spain — Nico Williams OUT / Yeremy Pino doubt (M83)**: Spain lose their left-side burst. Market still prices Spain as a near-lock vs Austria; the *scoreline* is tighter than priced (Spain were 0-0, 1-0 grinders even at full strength — 5 GF in 3 group games).
- **Egypt — Salah hamstring (M86)**: the entire Egypt price is a Salah price. If he's limited, Australia (Sarasota-camp fitness, dome neutralising any heat) are live. This is the most two-way mispriced match on the card.
- **Brazil — Raphinha doubt (M74)**: Brazil's set-piece + width creativity drops; Japan already beat this Brazil 1-0 in a friendly. Houston dome removes Brazil's historical heat-acclimatisation edge — a quiet negative for the favourite the market ignores.
- **Netherlands — de Ligt/de Vrij/Timber/Schouten all OUT (M76)**: a top-4-priced side with a third-choice central defence.

**4. Rest/travel differential is mild this round** (most sides finished R3 Jun 24–26, R32 spread Jun 28–Jul 3) — I only flag it where it compounds (South Africa: 3 games in ~2 weeks for a squad unused to the level, now a 4th; DR Congo & Ghana similar debutant-fatigue).

**5. Default knockout shape:** Tier-1 R32 base rates (analogised from Euro R16): ~70% favourite-advance, ~30–35% ET, ~15–18% to penalties. Knockout games run ~0.3 goals lower than group games. So I lean to **tighter scorelines than group output** and reserve draws for genuinely level or two-strong-defence ties.

## Top 3 matches where conditions/market factors most shift the outcome vs pure stats

1. **M79 Mexico vs Ecuador (Azteca altitude) — my flagship divergence.** Pure stats lean Mexico (home, in-form). The conditions/market read flips it toward a draw/Ecuador-shootout: altitude is *neutralised* by Ecuador being the one altitude-trained opponent, the crowd over-prices Mexico on the altitude narrative, Ecuador's defence is elite, and the "fifth-game curse" + PSO temperament favours Ecuador. The market's Mexico price is the biggest single overvaluation on the board.

2. **M86 Australia vs Egypt (Salah hamstring + dome) — the realest two-way gap.** Pure stats (group form) favour Egypt. But the dome erases Egypt's heat edge while Australia's Sarasota camp leaves them fitter, and a limited Salah guts Egypt's attack. The market is anchored to a healthy-Salah Egypt; a 1-1-to-pens with Australia live is the underpriced outcome.

3. **M76 Netherlands vs Morocco (Monterrey NIGHT game) — the conditions head-fake.** The crowd will price "Monterrey heat → Morocco," but the 9 PM kickoff strips the heat, so the actual edge is Morocco's compact block exploiting a Netherlands defence missing four first-choice defenders — pushing this from a comfortable Dutch win toward the underpriced 1-1-to-penalties bucket. Correctly identifying that the conditions narrative is *muted by kickoff time* is the whole value here.
