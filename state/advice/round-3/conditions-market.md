# Round 3 advice — conditions-market lens

> Lens priority: venue/altitude/heat/travel + bookmaker-vs-stats mispricing gaps above pure form. R3 is where conditions bite hardest — three altitude games (Mexico City 2240m, Guadalajara 1560m ×2) and four open-air heat games (Miami ×1, Kansas City ×2 outdoor) split the card. The biggest single edge in the whole round is **Uruguay's worst-in-tournament environmental sequence finishing at altitude vs the best heat-adapted European squad**.

## Group A

## Czechia vs Mexico @ Mexico City (alt ~2240m): 1-2
- Toto: away win (Mexico)
- Confidence: high
- Why (through the conditions lens): Azteca 2240m is the decisive factor — Czechia's entire squad is German/PL/La Liga sea-level based with zero altitude exposure, and visiting teams systematically fade in the third quarter at this height. Mexico (6 pts, clinched) are home, altitude-acclimatised and unbeaten in 8; they need the win to secure top seed and have the structural edge the crowd underrates. Czechia's set-piece threat (Schick, Krejci) gives them a goal, but altitude + home crowd points the toto firmly to Mexico.

## South Africa vs South Korea @ Guadalupe/Guadalajara (alt ~1560m): 1-2
- Toto: away win (South Korea)
- Confidence: med
- Why (through the conditions lens): Guadalajara's 1560m is a mild altitude both sea-level squads must absorb, so it neutralises rather than tilts — leaving form/standings as the read. South Korea (3 pts, needs win to advance) carry Son and clearly superior qualifying pedigree (40 GF); South Africa (0 pts, eliminated-track, Foster/Williams fitness clouds) are playing for pride. Korea's transition pace at altitude vs a tiring SA block gives a 2-1.
