# Round 3 advice — contrarian lens

Lens rule: lock the most defensible toto (protect the 60), then chase the underpriced *scoreline* inside it (the +30). Round 3 is the final group round — standings context (who is through, who must win, who chases GD) drives the score shape. Where a side is already qualified I lean rotation/coast; where a side must win I push their numbers. Bold-but-defensible scoreline shapes preferred over chalk.

## Czechia vs Mexico @ Mexico City (alt ~2240m): 1-2
- Toto: away win
- Confidence: med
- Why (through the contrarian lens): Mexico clinched-adjacent at top (6 pts) but plays at home Azteca altitude — they go for the win to top the group; Czechia (3 pts, 3rd) must win to be sure of advancing, so they push and score from set pieces (Schick/Krejci) but a sea-level squad fades at 2240m late. The crowd defaults to a tidy 0-2; the value shape is Czechia scoring from a corner then conceding twice — 1-2 banks the away-win toto with an underpriced scoreline.
